Overview

The Society of Statisticians & Actuaries (SSA) is a long-standing student organization within SDSU’s statistics department. I joined as a first-year student and held multiple leadership roles as the organization transitioned through rebuilding phases.


Unlike founding an organization, SSA placed me inside an existing structure, where leadership meant working within constraints, maintaining continuity, and adapting systems rather than designing them from scratch.

Roles Held

  • Event Coordinator (Aug 2023 – Dec 2023)
  • Executive Vice President (Dec 2023 – Aug 2024)
  • Executive Secretary (Aug 2024 – May 2025)

What I Accomplished

Programming & Academic Support

  • Recruited guest speakers from Google, UCLA, Brown University, and SDSU alumni
  • Coordinated academic support sessions, including exam reviews and study groups
  • Led STAT 350A study sessions as a first-year student

Operations & Outreach

  • Supported event logistics and coordination
  • Assisted cross-organization outreach and promotion
  • Tracked attendance trends through sign-in data

Scale & Context

  • 180+ historical members
  • ~25 attendees during stable periods
  • ~15 during rebuilding phases
  • <10 during later cohort transitions

Despite fluctuating engagement, SSA remained a consistent academic and professional bridge between coursework and applied statistics.

Leadership Growth

SSA was my first experience leading within an established institutional structure. Instead of building systems from scratch, I learned how to operate within constraints, maintain continuity, and support existing organizational identity.


This environment forced me to focus less on creation and more on sustainability: what happens when leadership is inherited rather than designed.

How This Shaped What Came Next

My experience in SSA directly informed the creation of the Data Science Initiative (DSI). SSA represented applied statistics and actuarial identity within SDSU, while I saw a parallel need for a data science-focused community.


DSI was shaped in part by lessons learned here: structure matters, continuity matters, and organizations evolve through stewardship as much as innovation.
