Why I Got Involved

I helped establish the GrowLuv SDSU Chapter to create a space centered on mental wellness, care, and genuine student connection.


At the time, I was heavily involved in technical and professional leadership roles. I wanted to build something that did not revolve around performance, output, or optimization— but instead focused on people.


GrowLuv was intentionally designed to be a space where students could exist without pressure to achieve, prove, or perform.

What I Did

  • Co-founded the SDSU chapter of GrowLuv as part of a national mental wellness nonprofit
  • Helped shape early structure, values, and community norms
  • Supported student-led engagement focused on connection over metrics
  • Assisted in lightweight organizational tracking while preserving psychological safety

Impact and Scope

  • Engaged 70+ students through community-based events and conversations
  • Created a low-pressure environment for student connection
  • Helped establish an alternative model of leadership centered on care

What I Learned

GrowLuv reinforced that not every meaningful space benefits from optimization or heavy measurement.


I learned that leadership sometimes means:

  • Knowing when not to measure
  • Allowing culture to form organically
  • Stepping back once a space is stable

This experience shaped how I think about sustainability, boundaries, and people-centered leadership.

How This Fits Into My Work

GrowLuv complements my technical and organizational leadership experiences by grounding them in empathy and care.


It reinforces a core principle in my broader work: effective systems are not only built to scale output, but also to sustain people.
