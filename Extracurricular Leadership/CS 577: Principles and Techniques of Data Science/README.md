# CS 577: Principles and Techniques of Data Science — San Diego State University

**Role:** Instructional Student Assistant & Curriculum Co-Developer  
**Timeframe:** May 2026 – July 2026  
**Location:** San Diego, CA  

## Why This Role Matters

After I founded the Data Science Initiative at SDSU and passed the baton to the future leaders of data science at SDSU, aka DataX, I stepped down from student leadership to focus on CS 577, a class I took myself in Spring 2026. Following Spring 2026, I realized that becoming an ISA to co-develop a growing curriculum felt like a natural continuation of the same work: helping students build practical data science skills, only in a more structured academic setting.

CS 577 is an upper-division and graduate-level course focused on the principles, methods, and workflows used in modern data science. As an Instructional Student Assistant, I support the course by helping develop materials, assignments, and learning resources for students.

## What I’m Building

### 1) Course materials and curriculum support
- Co-developing instructional materials for **CS 577: Principles and Techniques of Data Science**
- Helping organize topics around core data science concepts, applied workflows, and technical skill development
- Supporting the creation of clear examples, assignments, and learning resources

### 2) Applied data science learning
- Helping students connect data science concepts to practical implementation

### 3) Student-centered instructional support
- Assisting undergraduate and graduate students with course concepts, tools, and project work
- Helping identify where students may need clearer explanations or additional structure
- Bridging the gap between technical implementation and conceptual understanding

## Leadership Decisions and Tradeoffs

This role is different from founding DSI because it is not about creating a new organization from scratch. Instead, I'm contributing to an existing academic course where clarity, structure, and student learning outcomes matter most.

Some tradeoffs I’m learning to navigate:
- **Clarity over complexity:** Strong teaching means knowing what to simplify, not just what to include
- **Structure over improvisation:** Course materials need to be scoped clearly so students can build skills step by step
- **Support over spotlight:** Good instructional work often happens in the background, through better examples, clearer assignments, and stronger learning support

## What I’m Learning
- How to design educational experiences that balance conceptual understanding with technical implementation
- Curriculum development principles, including sequencing topics, scaffolding skills, and defining learning outcomes
- The challenges of teaching data science to students with varying levels of programming, statistics, and domain knowledge
- How to translate complex technical ideas into explanations that are accurate, approachable, and actionable
- The importance of iteration in education—small improvements to assignments, examples, and instructions can significantly improve student outcomes
- How instructional design and data science share similar goals: creating systems that help people make better decisions with information


## How CS 577 Connects to My Work Now

CS 577 builds directly on the work I started through DSI. DSI created a student-led pathway into data science; CS 577 allows me to support data science learning inside the classroom.

Together, these experiences reflect the same core theme in my work: I care about building analytical systems, but I also care about building the people and learning environments that make those systems useful.
