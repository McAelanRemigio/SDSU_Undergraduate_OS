# Music Performance & Storytelling

**Role:** Solo Singer, Duet Performer, Group Performer  
**Overall Timeframe:** Childhood – Present  
**SDSU Timeframe:** 2023 – 2024  
**Location:** San Diego State University

## Why This Matters To Me

Music has been part of my life for as long as I can remember.

Growing up in a Filipino household, singing was everywhere. Family gatherings, celebrations, karaoke nights, and community events all seemed to find their way back to music. Long before I understood performance, I understood that songs could bring people together.

When I arrived at San Diego State University, music became one of the ways I stayed connected to that part of myself.

Unlike my work in data science, research, or student leadership, music was something I pursued simply because I loved it. It gave me an outlet for creativity, self-expression, and storytelling in a way that spreadsheets, reports, and code never could.

One thing remained consistent throughout nearly every performance I chose: romance.

While I participated in a variety of musical performances, the songs I selected as a soloist almost always revolved around love, connection, and relationships. Romance has always been a subject that resonates deeply with me because it reflects some of the most meaningful experiences people can share. Through music, I found a way to tell those stories and connect with audiences through emotions that are nearly universal.

## What I Did

### 1) Campus Performances

Throughout my time at SDSU, I performed at a variety of student events as both a soloist and collaborative performer.

These included:

- Aztec Night Market
- APSA Formal
- Battle of the Bands
- Cultural showcases and student organization events

Across these performances, I sang for audiences ranging from approximately 100 to more than 500 attendees.

One of my performances was featured on SDSU's official social media, highlighting student talent and campus involvement.

### 2) Solo and Duet Performance

While I participated in group numbers, my favorite performances were always solo and duet acts.

My repertoire frequently included:

- Jazz standards
- Crooner-style music
- Swing influences
- Romantic ballads

These performances challenged me to develop:

- Stage presence
- Audience engagement
- Vocal confidence
- Live performance skills
- Emotional storytelling

Standing alone on a stage and holding the attention of a crowd taught me a different kind of confidence than leadership positions or classroom presentations ever could.

### 3) Competitive Performance

In 2024, I competed as a featured act in a campus talent competition.

Notable accomplishments include:

- Selected as a Top 4 competing act
- Performed before an audience of 500+ attendees
- Earned Runner-Up placement

Preparing for competition taught me how to transform a song into a complete performance, combining musical preparation, stage presence, and audience connection.

## Outcomes I'm Proud Of

- Performed publicly across multiple SDSU events
- Sang before audiences ranging from 100 to 500+ attendees
- Earned Runner-Up placement in a campus talent competition
- Continued a lifelong passion for music throughout college
- Developed confidence performing as a solo vocalist
- Shared stories and emotions through music that resonated with audiences

## What I Learned

Music taught me that communication is not only about information.

The best performances are not necessarily the most technically perfect. They are the ones that make people feel something.

Whether singing, presenting research, teaching students, or leading organizations, the goal is often the same: connect with people in a meaningful way.

Music also taught me how to be vulnerable.

Performing a song in front of hundreds of people requires a willingness to be seen. It means putting something personal into the world and accepting that not everyone will respond the same way. Learning to do that made me more confident both on and off the stage.

## How This Connects To My Work Today

Although music may seem unrelated to data science, many of the same skills continue to influence my academic and professional work:

- Public speaking
- Storytelling
- Audience awareness
- Performance under pressure
- Preparation and practice
- Confidence in front of large groups

Whether I am presenting research findings, teaching students, leading a team, or performing on stage, I am ultimately trying to accomplish the same thing: communicate ideas in a way that people remember.
