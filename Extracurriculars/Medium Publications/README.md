# Writing & Reflective Publications

**Role:** Writer, Essayist  
**Platform:** Medium  
**Publication:** The Ink Home  
**Timeframe:** 2025 – Present  
**Location:** San Diego State University

## Why This Matters To Me

Writing became one of the most important ways I processed my undergraduate experience.

While much of my time at SDSU was spent in data science, leadership, and performance-based extracurriculars, writing gave me a space to slow down and reflect on everything happening in real time. It allowed me to take experiences that were often fast-paced and outcome-driven and turn them into structured understanding.

Over time, writing became less about documentation and more about interpretation — understanding what experiences meant, how they shaped me, and how they connected to broader themes in my life.

Many of my essays focus on identity, leadership, presence, and personal growth during college.

## What I Did

### 1) Long-Form Essay Writing

Since 2025, I have written and published reflective essays on Medium covering personal, academic, and professional experiences throughout college.

These essays explore themes such as:

- Leadership and organizational identity  
- Academic acceleration and unconventional college paths  
- Burnout, presence, and personal development  
- Performance vs. authenticity  
- Navigating growth during a high-pressure academic environment  

My writing style is narrative-driven and reflective, focusing on meaning-making rather than technical or academic analysis.

## What I Learned

Writing taught me how to slow down experience.

In environments focused on execution — such as research, leadership, and performance — it is easy to move from one milestone to the next without fully processing what each moment means.

Writing gave me the ability to step outside of that cycle and reflect.

It also taught me that understanding is not automatic. It is something built through deliberate reflection over time.

## How This Connects To My Work Today

Although writing may seem separate from my technical and leadership work, it directly reinforces the same core skills:

- Structured thinking  
- Communication and storytelling  
- Reflection and self-assessment  
- Translating complexity into clarity  
- Understanding systems through narrative  

Whether in data science, leadership, performance, or writing, I am ultimately working with the same goal: understanding systems and communicating them in meaningful ways.
